# didLibrary
The Digital Intellectual Data (DID) Library's primary goal is to ensure that all stakeholders, including creators, artists, producers, and consumers, forever have fair and equal access to digital media information and is a single entity that represents multitudes of serverless digital libraries, data warehouse and databases, focused on organizing, preserving, accessing vast amounts of digital media information; integrating intellectual property (IP) law, royalties data analytics, and artist/producer privacy rights. To manage resources like academic journals, copyrights, digitized artificats such as historical documents alongwith multimedia assets; by utilizing AI for discovering and combating challenges in licensing, ownership and reconcillation to ensure equitable access across domains.
